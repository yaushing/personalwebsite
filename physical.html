I AM ... A SPARTAN KID

"It is exercise alone that supports the spirit and keeps the mind in vigor"

Cicero

The ancient civilisation of Sparta practices the tradition where all children of Sparta are responsible to serve its government. For that purpose alone, a child of Sparta needs to be strong and a fast learner just to survive. The Spartan government prides strength, power and mental capability to bring their civilisation to new heights.

THE SPARTAN KIDS CODE

  • A Spartan thinks positively and is resilient through adversity
  • A Spartan helps others and asks for help when they need it
  • A Spartan understands that eating a healthy diet and exercising often keeps our bodies strong
  • A Spartan gets rest to help their bodies recover and grow
  • A Spartan understands their current abilities
  • A Spartan sets new goals to be the best that they can be
My Journey
Workouts
Year 2022

With the countinous easing of restrictions significantly increasing the sports I could try, I was excited to try new sports. From water sports like Paddleboarding to team-based or individual-based Tennis, I am sure that this year will be amazing.

Year 2021

The easing of restrictions significantly increased the sports I could try. Although I will be sitting for my Primary Six Leaving Examination, I alway make sure to get enough exercise.

Year 2020

The COVID19 pandemic has changed how I continue onwards with my journey in fitness and health. I mostly clock 5km of biking or 3km of running around my neighborhood utilizing strava to keep me in check. I have come to appreciate physical fitness as a prerequisite to for the endurance of mental prowess.

Year 2019

2019 was a year where I ventured into various other sporting activity aside from my training in BJJ and swimming, from team based football and badminton to individual focused sports such as figure skating and rock climbing!

Year 2018

I started training in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) as a form of fitness and self defence on top of swimming. I've come to appreaciate that both of these are lifeskills for survival. My most crucial take away from BJJ are the values of compassion, bravery, honesty and humility; values which forms the ethics and moral code of this martial art form.
